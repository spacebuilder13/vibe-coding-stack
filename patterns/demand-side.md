# Pattern: Demand-Side Framing

Position every app by the struggling moment it resolves — not by its features.

## The core question

"What is the user trying to do that isn't working right now?"
Not: "What does this app do?" but: "What painful moment does it interrupt?"

## The 4 Forces (from Demand-Side Sales)

1. **Push** — frustration with the current situation pushing them to act
2. **Pull** — attraction to the new solution
3. **Anxiety** — fear that the new solution won't work
4. **Habit** — comfort with the existing way holding them back

For a 1-shot brief to work, Push must be stronger than Habit + Anxiety.

## Segment template

For each target user, define:

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **Segment name** | S1 — New Parent |
| **Struggling moment** | "I just had a baby and realised I have no life cover" |
| **Current bad solution** | Googling, asking friends, being sold to by agents |
| **What they actually want** | Honest answer: do I have enough cover, and what should I do first? |
| **Anxiety about your app** | "Will it try to sell me something?" |
| **How you beat the habit** | No commission, no selling, just answer |

## Applied examples (from project-insurance)

| Segment | Struggling moment | One next action |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| S1 — New Parent | "I have a baby and no term plan" | Get a ₹1Cr term plan quote today |
| S2 — Mis-insured | "I paid premiums for 10 years and found out my cover is worthless" | Audit what you have |
| S3 — Job changer | "My corporate cover expires in 48 hours" | Know what to buy before Monday |
| S4 — Aging parent caregiver | "My parents' cover just lapsed" | Find out what options remain |

## In PROGRAM.md

The "Who is it for?" field should always be a struggling moment, not a demographic.

Bad: "For 25-35 year old urban professionals"
Good: "For someone who just realised their insurance might not actually pay out"

## App store listing copy

Use the same framing in `app-store/STORE.md`:
- Name: functional, not clever
- Description: struggling moment → what the app does → one next action
